# Growth Log Template

> Use this template to record evidence of your engineering growth. One entry per meaningful session or milestone. Over time, this log becomes the source-of-truth artifact for your promotion packet.

---

## How to Use

1. Copy the **Entry Template** section below into your own `GROWTH_LOG.md` file (keep it local or in a private repo).
2. Fill it in immediately after a Career Catalyst session — the agent generates the **Agent-Generated Evidence** section for you.
3. Review your log quarterly. Look for patterns in your Thinking Shifts; they tell the story of your level progression.

> **Privacy:** This file is for you. Do not commit entries containing proprietary system names, internal metrics, or confidential business context to a public repository.

## Quarterly Review Prompts

Use these prompts every quarter to synthesize your entries into a promotion narrative:

1. **Pattern recognition:** What type of problem keeps appearing in my log? Am I expanding my scope, or working in the same lane?
2. **Level gap:** Based on my entries and the rubric, what level am I demonstrably operating at vs. where I want to be?
3. **Influence evidence:** In how many entries did I accelerate another engineer's work or shape a decision beyond my team?
4. **Biggest thinking shift:** Which single entry represents the largest jump in how I approached a problem?
5. **Promotion readiness:** If I had to write a 3-sentence promotion argument from this log today, what would it be?

## Example: Completed Entry

---

### Entry: Designed observability strategy for distributed checkout pipeline

**Date:** 2025-03-12

**Context:**
Three separate teams owned different legs of the checkout pipeline (cart, payments, fulfillment). Incidents frequently required 45+ minute cross-team debugging sessions because there was no shared trace context. I was asked to "fix the logging."

---

**Thinking Shift:**

- **From:** Senior (L3) — *"I should add better logging to the checkout service I own."*
- **To:** Staff (L4) — *"I need to define a platform-wide trace propagation contract so that any engineer in any of these three services can follow a request end-to-end without knowing the other systems."*

---

**Technical Outcome:**
Designed and drove adoption of a shared `X-Trace-Context` header standard across all three services. Wrote the RFC, ran three design reviews with each team's tech lead, and built the reference implementation. Mean time to diagnose checkout incidents dropped from 45 min to 8 min within 30 days of rollout.
