# Default Engineering Leveling Rubric

> This rubric defines the default career ladder for Career Catalyst. Replace or supplement it with your company's internal framework using the [Private Rubric Protocol](../README.md#private-rubric-protocol-byor).

---

## Philosophy

The primary differentiator across levels is **Scope of Impact** — not years of experience, not the size of PRs, not the number of systems touched. Impact scope answers: *how far does your thinking, your decisions, and your work reach?*

The progression is:

**Task → Feature → System → Platform → Strategy**

Each level up requires you to carry the *full weight* of the previous scope while extending your thinking to the next.

---

## Leveling Table

| Level | Title | Scope of Impact | Technical Expectations | Leadership & Influence | Typical Mindset Anchors |
|-------|-------|----------------|------------------------|------------------------|-------------------------|
| **L1** | Junior Engineer | **Task** — Works on well-defined, bounded tasks with close guidance. Rarely owns more than a single function or file. | Writes correct code for specified requirements. Asks for help proactively. Learns debugging practices. | Receives mentorship. Communicates blockers clearly. | "How do I make this work?" |
| **L2** | Mid-Level Engineer | **Feature** — Owns end-to-end delivery of a feature within an existing system. Understands how pieces connect. | Designs and implements full features. Considers edge cases and error handling. Writes tests. Reviews peers' code. | Begins mentoring L1s. Participates in design discussions. | "How do I ship this correctly and completely?" |
| **L3** | Senior Engineer | **System** — Owns one or more services or system boundaries. Can reason about trade-offs across a system. | Leads technical design for complex features. Sets quality standards. Drives incident response. Identifies and reduces technical debt strategically. | Leads project delivery. Mentors L1–L2s. Influences team technical direction. | "How do I build this to last and scale?" |
| **L4** | Staff Engineer | **Platform** — Shapes shared infrastructure, tooling, or architectural patterns used by multiple teams. | Architects solutions that span team boundaries. Defines and enforces platform-wide standards. Solves ambiguous, high-stakes problems. Balances velocity vs. resilience. | Partners with Engineering Managers. Drives cross-team alignment. Multiplies team effectiveness through systems and process. | "How does my work make every engineer on the platform more effective?" |
| **L5** | Senior Staff Engineer | **Strategy** — Defines the technical vision for an entire domain or organization. Decisions have multi-year impact. | Identifies and leads the most impactful technical investments (build vs. buy, platform evolution, capability gaps). Navigates org-level constraints. | Sets direction for Staff and below. Counsels senior leadership on technical strategy. Shapes hiring and org design. | "What is the right problem to be solving, and am I solving the right one for the business?" |

## Scope Differentiator Deep Dive

### Task (L1)
- Input: A ticket with acceptance criteria.
- Output: A passing implementation.
- Thinking horizon: hours to days.
- Risk horizon: "Does this break my change?"

### Feature (L2)
- Input: A product requirement or user story.
- Output: A shippable, tested feature.
- Thinking horizon: days to weeks.
- Risk horizon: "Does this break the user journey?"

### System (L3)
- Input: A business problem or architectural gap.
- Output: A designed, reviewed, and deployed system improvement.
- Thinking horizon: weeks to months.
- Risk horizon: "Does this create reliability, security, or operational debt?"

### Platform (L4)
- Input: A recurring pain point across multiple teams or a strategic capability gap.
- Output: A platform-level abstraction, standard, or service that teams can build on.
- Thinking horizon: quarters.
- Risk horizon: "Does this constrain or unlock the organization's ability to move?"

### Strategy (L5)
- Input: Business goals, competitive pressures, org capabilities.
- Output: A multi-year technical roadmap and organizational alignment behind it.
- Thinking horizon: years.
- Risk horizon: "Are we building the right things to win?"

## Behavioral Anchors: Senior vs. Staff (The Common Jump)

The L3 → L4 transition is the most commonly misunderstood jump in the industry. Here is the core shift:

| Dimension | Senior (L3) | Staff (L4) |
|-----------|------------|------------|
| **Problem framing** | Solves the problem as given | Questions whether it's the right problem |
| **Solution scope** | Best solution for their team | Best solution for the platform / adjacent teams |
| **Influence method** | Code + docs | Code + docs + standards + org patterns |
| **Incident response** | Fixes the thing that broke | Asks why the class of problem exists at all |
| **Meetings** | Attends to contribute | Attends to align and unblock |
| **Success metric** | My system is healthy | Teams I interact with ship faster and safer |
